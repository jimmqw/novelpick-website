Best Chinese Wuxia Web Novels 2026

Legendary Heroes, Martial Arts Epics, and Tales of Honor in the Jianghu

April 30, 2026•12 min read•By NovelPick

Wuxia — literally "martial heroes" — is one of the oldest and most beloved genres in Chinese fiction. It's the world of wandering swordsmen, hidden masters, forbidden techniques, and codes of honor that transcend life and death. Unlike xianxia (which leans into immortality and cosmic power), wuxia stays grounded in human-scale conflict — the martial world, or jianghu, where strength is earned through discipline and loyalty is the highest currency. In 2026, web novel platforms are producing some of the finest wuxia storytelling the genre has ever seen. These are the ten that deserve your attention.

1. Sword of Coming (剑来) by Feng Huo Xi Zhu Hou

#1 Sword of Coming Ongoing
Platform: Qidian / Webnovel • Chapters: 1,200+
Chen Ping'an was just an ordinary young man from a small town, content to make ceramics and live a quiet life. Then a wandering swordsman passed through, and everything changed. Sword of Coming is arguably the most literary wuxia web novel ever written — Feng Huo's prose is dense with classical Chinese philosophy, and every character, from the protagonist to the innkeeper who appears for three chapters, feels alive with their own motivations. It's a slow burn, but for readers who want wuxia as literature rather than popcorn entertainment, nothing else comes close.
For readers who want: Philosophical depth, morally complex characters, and prose that rewards rereading.
Classical Wuxia Slow Burn Sword Cultivation Philosophical

2. Martial World (武极天下) by Cocooned Cow

#2 Martial World Complete
Platform: Qidian / Webnovel • Chapters: 2,256
If you want the definitive modern wuxia reading experience, Martial World is it. Lin Ming starts as a talented but disadvantaged youth from a fading martial family, armed with nothing but a mysterious cube left by his ancestor and an indomitable will. Cocooned Cow takes the classic wuxia formula — young master rises through tournament arcs, secret realm explorations, and increasingly powerful enemies — and executes it with such momentum and craft that it becomes impossible to put down. The world-building is deep, the power system is internally consistent, and the pacing never lets up across more than 2,000 chapters. It's the gold standard for progression wuxia.
For readers who want: The perfect entry point into wuxia, with flawless pacing and an iconic protagonist.
Progression Fantasy Tournament Arc Body Tempering Complete

3. A Will Eternal (一念永恒) by Er Gen

#3 A Will Eternal Complete
Platform: Qidian / Webnovel • Chapters: 1,314
Bai Xiaochun is terrified of death — so terrified, in fact, that he'll do absolutely anything to live forever. This leads him to develop increasingly absurd techniques, cook pills that explode, and charm or annoy his way through every sect he joins. Er Gen is best known for his sprawling cultivation epics, but A Will Eternal is his most accessible and entertaining work — it's genuinely funny in a way few wuxia novels manage, while still delivering the grand-scale battles and emotional stakes the genre demands. If you've ever felt that wuxia takes itself too seriously, start here.
For readers who want: Laugh-out-loud comedy alongside genuine martial arts progression and heart.
Comedy Wuxia Alchemy Sect Life Complete

4. The Legend of the Condor Heroes (射雕英雄传) by Jin Yong

#4 The Legend of the Condor Heroes Complete
Platform: Multiple publishers • Originally published 1957
No wuxia list is complete without Jin Yong, the genre's undisputed master. The Legend of the Condor Heroes follows Guo Jing, a simple but honest young man raised on the Mongolian steppes, as he stumbles into a conflict between the Song Empire and the invading Jin. What makes this novel endure across nearly 70 years isn't the martial arts (though the fight scenes are excellent) — it's the characters. Guo Jing's journey from slow-witted orphan to national hero, his love story with the clever Huang Rong, and the deeply human villains who believe they're doing the right thing. There is a reason this has been adapted into over a dozen films and television series.
For readers who want: The foundational classic of the genre, timeless characters, and a love story that defined wuxia romance.
Classic Masterpiece Historical Romance Complete

5. Reverend Insanity (ŜGal;人) by Gu Zhen Ren

#5 Reverend Insanity Ongoing (Hiatus)
Platform: Qidian • Chapters: 2,334
Fang Yuan is not a hero. He's not even an anti-hero in the traditional sense — he's a man who has lived 500 years of a previous life, learned that virtue is weakness, and now pursues eternal life with cold, calculating ruthlessness. Reverend Insanity is controversial for good reason: the protagonist commits genuinely disturbing acts, and the novel doesn't flinch from showing the consequences. But it's also the most intellectually ambitious wuxia web novel ever written, with a power system based on Gu insects that functions like a dark, Darwinian ecosystem. For readers tired of noble protagonists who always do the right thing, this is the antidote.
For readers who want: A genuinely amoral protagonist, a brilliantly original power system, and zero hand-holding.
Dark Wuxia Villain MC Gu System Mature

6. The Smiling, Proud Wanderer (笑傲江湖) by Jin Yong

#6 The Smiling, Proud Wanderer Complete
Platform: Multiple publishers • Originally published 1967
Linghu Chong is the kind of protagonist that feels modern even today: a free-spirited swordsman who loves wine, music, and doing what he believes is right — regardless of what his sect, his peers, or society demands. The Smiling, Proud Wanderer is Jin Yong's most subversive wuxia novel, a story about how the martial world's obsession with power and prestige corrupts everyone it touches. The sword techniques — especially the legendary Nine Swords of Dugu — are iconic, but the real masterpiece is Linghu Chong's relationship with the mysterious Ren Yingying, and the novel's meditation on what it means to be free in a world that demands conformity.
For readers who want: A free-spirited hero, political intrigue in the martial world, and the most iconic sword technique in wuxia.
Classic Masterpiece Political Intrigue Romance Complete

7. World of Cultivation (修真世界) by Fang Xiang

#7 World of Cultivation Complete
Platform: Qidian • Chapters: 915
Zuo Mo wakes up with a strange face on his forehead, no memories of his past, and one goal: earn enough spirit stones to buy his sect's land back from debt collectors. What follows is one of the most unique cultivation stories in the genre, blending wuxia with economic strategy, sect management, and large-scale warfare. Fang Xiang's world is intricate and lived-in — cultivation is treated as a profession with specializations (sword cultivators, spell cultivators, body cultivators, formation masters), and Zuo Mo's journey from desperate sect disciple to faction leader feels earned at every step. The supporting cast is exceptional, particularly the comic-relief sword spirit Pu Yao and the stoic corpse general Wei Sheng.
For readers who want: Strategic depth, sect-building, and a cultivation system that feels like a real profession.
Sect Building Strategy Warfare Complete

8. I Shall Be Sealed Heaven (我欲封天) by Er Gen

#8 I Shall Be Sealed Heaven Complete
Platform: Qidian / Webnovel • Chapters: 1,613
Xu Qing lives in a world where gods walk among mortals and humanity lives in constant fear. He starts at the absolute bottom — a scavenger in the ruins of a dead god's body — and through sheer grit and the discovery of a mysterious violet crystal, begins his climb toward something resembling hope. Er Gen's latest completed work (and arguably his best since I Shall Seal the Heavens) returns to the dark, oppressive atmosphere of his earlier novels. The world of the Revered Ancient era is brutal and magnificent in equal measure, and Xu Qing's transformation from scavenger to something far more is among the most satisfying progression arcs in modern wuxia.
For readers who want: A dark, atmospheric world, a protagonist who earns every scrap of power, and Er Gen at his best.
Dark Fantasy Gods & Mortals Atmospheric Complete

9. Dragon King With Seven Stars (七星龙王) by Gu Long

#9 Dragon King With Seven Stars Complete
Platform: Multiple publishers • Originally published 1978
Gu Long is Jin Yong's great rival, and where Jin Yong writes epic historical sagas, Gu Long writes noir detective stories in the martial world. Dragon King With Seven Stars opens with a dead man found in an impossible location and spirals into a conspiracy involving the most powerful figures in the jianghu. The protagonist, Yuan Suiyun, is a blind martial artist who compensates for his disability with supernatural hearing and an extraordinary deductive mind. Gu Long's prose is terse, his dialogue crackles, and his fight scenes are resolved in single, decisive exchanges rather than extended technique-fests. It's unlike anything else on this list — and essential reading for understanding the full range of what wuxia can be.
For readers who want: A wuxia noir detective story, minimalist prose, and a protagonist who thinks rather than smashes.
Wuxia Noir Mystery Classic Masterpiece Complete

10. Ze Tian Ji (择天记 / Way of Choices) by Mao Ni

#10 Ze Tian Ji (Way of Choices) Complete
Platform: Qidian / Webnovel • Chapters: 1,184
Chen Changsheng was born with an illness that guarantees he won't live past twenty. Rather than accept his fate, he decides to change it — by joining the most prestigious martial academy in the world and finding a way to defy the heavens themselves. Mao Ni writes with a literary sensibility rare in web novels: every chapter title is a line of classical poetry, and the philosophical debates between characters carry as much weight as the sword fights. Ze Tian Ji is a slow, meditative read that builds toward devastating emotional payoffs. The romance between Chen Changsheng and Xu Yourong is one of the most beautifully rendered relationships in the entire genre — two people who are destined to be enemies but choose each other anyway.
For readers who want: Literary prose, a doomed protagonist fighting fate, and a romance that will break your heart before it mends it.
Literary Wuxia Tragic Romance Academy Life Complete

Wuxia vs. Xianxia — A quick guide for newcomers: Wuxia is grounded martial arts with human-scale conflicts (think Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon). Xianxia adds immortality, godlike power, and cosmic stakes (think Chinese mythology meets Dragon Ball Z). This list focuses on wuxia and wuxia-adjacent works where martial skill and human choices matter more than heavenly tribulations and immortal realms.

NovelPick's Verdict

Start with Martial World if you're new to wuxia — it's the most accessible entry point and the gold standard for the genre's core appeal. Graduate to Sword of Coming or Ze Tian Ji when you're ready for literary ambition. And if you want to understand why wuxia has endured for centuries, read Jin Yong's The Legend of the Condor Heroes — it's the novel that made an entire culture fall in love with martial arts fiction.
