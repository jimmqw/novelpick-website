10 Books Like Cradle Series

📅 四月 7, 2026⏰ 7 min read
April 7, 2026 | 8 min read

Few book series have defined a genre quite like Cradle by Will Wight. Fast-paced progression, intricate magic systems, humor that lands, and a protagonist you can't help but root for �� Lindon goes from being the weakest person in his village to one of the most powerful beings in existence. If you've devoured all twelve books and find yourself in withdrawal, this list is for you.

What Makes Cradle Special

Before diving in, let's identify what you're actually craving: Is it the structured magic systems (sacred arts with clear rules and advancement tiers)? The satisfying power growth where every breakthrough feels earned? The humor that balances dark stakes? The romance? Or the battle-focused storytelling? Each recommendation below targets a different aspect of what makes Cradle great.

?? Top Recommendations

1. Mage Errant by John Bierce

by John Bierce (5 books, completed)

Closest to Cradle in spirit. Magic school, underdog protagonist, intricate magic systems, and found family. The magic system is particularly deep �� each student's power depends on their individual binding of magical creatures. It's like Cradle meets Harry Potter, with genuinely surprising plot twists.

Magic SchoolUnderdogCompleted

2. Dungeon Crawler Carl by Matt Dinniman

by Matt Dinniman (7 books, ongoing)

Dark humor meets LitRPG. Carl is thrust into a dungeon that's being broadcast as entertainment to the galaxy's elite. The Princess Donut is one of the best characters in the genre. Brutal, funny, and surprisingly emotional. Different tone from Cradle but equally addictive.

LitRPGDark HumorOngoing

3. Iron Prince by Bryce O'Connor

by Bryce O'Connor (Warformed series, ongoing)

Set in a world where people bond with sentient armor constructs. Reidon is disabled from birth and has to work twice as hard as everyone else. The magic system (Iron) is deeply technical and strategic. Power growth is slow and earned. Great for those who love Lindon's struggle.

MechaDisabled ProtagonistOngoing

4. The Wandering Inn by pirateaba

by pirateaba (ongoing, massive)

Don't let the initial premise (karaoke inn) throw you off. This is one of the most ambitious fantasy works ever written. Erin Solstice becomes an innkeeper in a LitRPG world. The world-building is unmatched, with hundreds of complex characters. Very long-form storytelling. Best if you want something to sink into for months.

LitRPGLong-formWorld-Building

5. Super Powereds by Drew Hayes

by Drew Hayes (4 books, completed)

College for superheroes, but with LitRPG-style power progression under the hood. The "Powereds" are students who have powers but not enough to be heroes. Found family, political intrigue, and satisfying growth arcs. Think Cradle's academy arc meets superhero genre.

SuperheroesAcademyCompleted

6. Defiance of the Fall by JF Brink

by JF Brink (ongoing, very long)

System apocalypse meets progression fantasy. Zac is one of the first to awaken in a world where everyone gets a System. Eastern flavor with cultivation-like advancement. Very fast pacing and satisfying combat. One of the most popular progression fantasy series right now.

System ApocalypseCultivationOngoing

7. Primal Hunter by Zogarth

by Zogarth (ongoing)

A System apocalypse litRPG with a protagonist who starts with a hunting-focused ability set. Jake evolves into increasingly powerful forms. The System mechanics are complex and interesting. Very fast pacing with plenty of action. Great for those who want power fantasy with minimal downtime.

SystemHunterOngoing

8. A Thousand Li by Tao Wong

by Tao Wong (ongoing, slow release)

Pure cultivation fantasy. Wu Long is a servant who discovers a cultivation manual and begins the long road to immortality. Slower, more contemplative pacing than Cradle, but deeply satisfying for its emphasis on the journey over the destination. If you love the "iron body foundation" parts of Cradle, read this.

CultivationSlow-burnOngoing

9. The Ripple System by Kyle Kirrin

by Kyle Kirrin (3 books, ongoing)

Negate, the protagonist, plays a VR game where he's literally cursed to be funny �� every joke he tells must land or he takes damage. It sounds silly but it's genuinely one of the most creative LitRPG premises out there. Fast-paced, hilarious, and surprisingly touching.

GamingHumorOngoing

10. Bastion by Phil Tucker

by Phil Tucker (ongoing)

A dark tower rises from the wasteland. Steli, the protagonist, is the lowest ranked "mote" who must climb the tower floor by floor. Incredible magic system, brutal world, and satisfying progression. More serious tone than Cradle but the power growth is equally addictive.

Tower ClimbDarkOngoing

Honorable Mentions

  • Mark of the Founder by Jon Kinnon �� farming-litRPG hybrid with a dragon protagonist
  • Full Murderhobo by Peter F. Downing �� dark humor, great characters
  • He Who Fights With Monsters by Shirtaloon �� Australian protagonist, great banter

Our Top Pick

If you want the closest Cradle experience, start with Mage Errant. If you want something more action-packed and darkly humorous, try Dungeon Crawler Carl. Both are completed or near-completed series �� no waiting on sequels. Happy reading!
