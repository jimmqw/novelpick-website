Meihua Yishu

Plum Blossom Numerology — divine any question from numbers, sounds, or images at the moment of inspiration. Elegant, fast, and remarkably accurate.

Meihua Yishu (梅花易数), "Plum Blossom Numerology," is one of the most elegant and accessible divination systems in Chinese metaphysics. Founded by Sung dynasty master Shao Yong, it allows you to derive a hexagram from virtually any input — numbers, sounds, words, even the way something catches your eye at an unexpected moment. This flexibility makes it uniquely powerful for quick, on-the-spot divination.

The Core Principle: Everything Is Data

Meihua rests on the philosophical foundation that all phenomena — numbers, images, sounds, timestamps — contain energetic information. When you observe something and feel drawn to divine about it, Meihua provides the framework to convert that observation into a meaningful hexagram. The classic example: if a bird flies past your window and you think "what does this mean?" — Meihua lets you use the bird's position, direction, and number of birds to construct a hexagram answer.

Data Sources: Your Divination Toolkit

Numbers

Any numbers — a birthdate, phone number, lottery number, or random number you choose. Divide into two parts for upper and lower hexagram trigrams.

Sounds

Any sound sequence — knocks on a door, bird calls, a car's horn pattern. Count the sounds to derive hexagram lines.

Images

Any image that draws your attention — the shape of a cloud, the arrangement of objects on a desk, a stranger's appearance. Interpret the image's elemental qualities.

Timestamps

The exact time (hour and minute) when the question arose becomes the input for the hexagram — clean and systematic.

Meihua vs. Liuyao

Liuyao requires a deliberate coin cast and structured ritual. Meihua is more spontaneous — any moment of inspiration can become a divination input. For quick questions where a formal ritual feels heavy, Meihua is ideal. For complex questions requiring full hexagram dynamics, Liuyao provides more structured depth.

The Meihua Process

The typical Meihua reading follows this pattern:

  • Observation: Something catches your attention and prompts a question — "What will happen if I take this meeting?"
  • Input: You note the immediate data — numbers around you, the time, an image you just saw.
  • Transformation: The data is converted into upper and lower trigrams, forming a hexagram.
  • Interpretation: The hexagram's meaning, combined with the changing lines, reveals the answer.

Strengths and Limitations

Meihua excels at quick, direct questions: "Should I go to this event?" "Is this person being honest?" "Where did I leave my keys?" Its elegance is in its immediacy — you don't need coins or stalks, just awareness and a moment.

It is less suited to complex, multi-dimensional questions requiring the full line-by-line analysis that Liuyao provides, or to long-term life mapping that BaZi and Ziwei handle best.

For the modern seeker, Meihua is an excellent daily practice — a quick check-in before important decisions that takes less than a minute once you understand the system.
