小六壬 · Little Six Cycle

Xiaoliuren

Quick daily divination from just three numbers — the fastest, most accessible Chinese metaphysics method for everyday decision support.

Xiaoliuren (小六壬, "Little Six Cycle") is the most accessible and immediately practical divination system in Chinese metaphysics. Using nothing more than the hour and minute of the moment you ask a question, you derive one of six palace positions — each with a clear, specific meaning. In under a minute, you have a answer: go or not go, trust or be cautious, act now or wait.

The Six Palaces: Your Quick Reference

Xiaoliuren uses a cyclical sequence of six positions. You calculate your position from the time, then read the corresponding palace meaning:

Da An
Large Safety · Index 1
The best outcome. Proceed confidently — things will unfold safely and favorably. Suitable for travel, new ventures, important meetings.
Xiao Kang
Small Safety · Index 2
Generally favorable, but with minor concerns. Proceed with light caution. Good for routine decisions and low-stakes situations.
Biandan
Urgent Correspondence · Index 3
Expect delays or correspondence requiring attention. Not the best time to act — tend to pending matters first.
Liulang
Wandering · Index 4
Uncertainty, restlessness, or travel indicated. The person or matter is in flux. Delay decisions if possible.
You Xiong
Hidden Mishap · Index 5
Hidden risks or potential problems not yet visible. Not recommended. If you must proceed, take extra precautions.
Wu Ji
Great Opportunity · Index 6
Major opportunity or turning point. Unexpected gains or favorable developments possible. Act with initiative.

How to Calculate

Xiaoliuren calculation uses the hour (1-12) and minute values of the moment you ask. The sum of hour plus minute plus day (modulo 6, with 0 becoming 6) determines your palace position. The sequence cycles: Da An → Xiao Kang → Biandan → Liulang → You Xiong → Wu Ji → back to Da An.

The Quick Formula

Index = (Hour + Minute + Day_of_Month) ÷ 6, remainder = position (1-6)

For example: asking at 3:47 on the 18th — (3 + 47 + 18) = 68 ÷ 6, remainder 2 → Xiao Kang (Small Safety).

Practical Daily Use

Xiaoliuren is perfect for quick daily decisions: "Should I take this call?" "Should I drive or take the train?" "Should I push for the signature today or wait?" It is not for complex life decisions — for those, BaZi, Liuyao, or Ziwei are appropriate. Xiaoliuren gives you a rapid signal check for everyday situations.

Applications

Daily Quick Checks

Should I take this meeting? Is this person being truthful? Xiaoliuren gives you a gut-level signal in under 30 seconds.

Travel Decisions

Is now the right time to travel? Should I take this route or that one? Quick safety indicators for daily movement.

Social Decisions

Should I accept this invitation? Is this person trustworthy? Quick interpersonal signals for everyday relationships.

Business Tactics

Should I make this call now? Is this the right moment to push? Xiaoliuren is a tactical timing tool for rapid decisions.

Xiaoliuren is the entry point into Chinese divination — simple enough to use immediately, meaningful enough to be worth trusting. For daily decision support, it is the most practical system available.
