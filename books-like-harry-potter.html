Missing Hogwarts? You're not alone. After finishing Harry Potter, many readers struggle to find that same feeling of wonder, friendship, and magical adventure. Here are 10 book series that capture similar elements.

1. The Magicians by Lev Grossman

★★★★?4.2/5

Why it's like Harry Potter: A secret magic school, but for adults. darker and more complex.

The twist: It's Harry Potter meets Narnia meets real-world consequences. Characters are flawed, magic has a price, and nothing is quite as whimsical as Hogwarts.

2. Nevermoor by Jessica Townsend

★★★★?4.6/5

Why it's like Harry Potter: A magical world hidden within the ordinary, a young outcast discovering their powers, and a whimsical boarding school setting.

Perfect for: Readers who loved the cozy, wonder-filled aspect of Harry Potter.

3. Percy Jackson by Rick Riordan

★★★★?4.7/5

Why it's like Harry Potter: A boy discovers he's special, goes to a camp for kids like him, and faces mythological adventures. Strong friendship dynamics and humor throughout.

The difference: Greek mythology instead of wizardry, but the same "found family" feeling.

4. The Scholomance by Naomi Novik

★★★★?4.5/5

Why it's like Harry Potter: A magic school, but everything wants to kill you. Dark, intense, and incredibly addictive.

Perfect for: Readers who wished Harry Potter was darker and had higher stakes.

5. The Name of the Wind by Patrick Rothfuss

★★★★?4.8/5

Why it's like Harry Potter: A talented young man attends a university for magic. Beautiful prose and deep world-building.

Warning: Series incomplete, but the first two books are masterpieces.

6. His Dark Materials by Philip Pullman

★★★★?4.6/5

Why it's like Harry Potter: Parallel worlds, daemons (animal companions), and a young protagonist facing epic challenges.

The difference: More philosophical and challenging to organized religion.

7. Cradle Series by Will Wight

★★★★?4.9/5

Why it's like Harry Potter: A young protagonist discovers a world of magic and cultivation, attending a school and growing in power.

Perfect for: Readers who loved the "learning magic" progression of Harry Potter.

8. Vita Nostra by Marina & Sergey Dyachenko

★★★★?4.3/5

Why it's like Harry Potter: A mysterious magic school in a remote location. But it's darker, more surreal, and deeply philosophical.

Warning: This is not a feel-good story. It's haunting and unforgettable.

9. Mother of Learning by nobody103

★★★★?4.9/5

Why it's like Harry Potter: A magic school, time loops, and a protagonist who must grow powerful to save everyone.

Perfect for: Fans of magical education and progression. Completed series!

10. Akata Witch by Nnedi Okorafor

★★★★?4.4/5

Why it's like Harry Potter: A young person discovers magical abilities, joins others like them, and faces a threat to their world.

The difference: Set in Nigeria with African mythology and culture.

Quick Recommendations

  • Want cozy and whimsical? ?Nevermoor
  • Want dark and mature? ?The Magicians or Scholomance
  • Want adventure and humor? ?Percy Jackson
  • Want progression and power fantasy? ?Cradle or Mother of Learning
  • Want beautiful prose? ?The Name of the Wind
