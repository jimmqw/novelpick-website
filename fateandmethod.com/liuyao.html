Liuyao Yijing

One of the oldest and most trusted Chinese divination systems — use six lines to gain precise, actionable answers to your most pressing questions.

Liuyao (六爻), meaning "Six Lines," is a systematic divination method that uses coins or yarrow stalks to generate six stacked lines — each either Yang (━) or Yin (━ ━) — forming a hexagram that maps directly onto the 64 hexagrams of the Yijing (易经, Classic of Changes). Unlike Yijing's more philosophical approach, Liuyao is purpose-built for answering specific questions with precision — from "Should I sign this contract?" to "Is this person trustworthy?" to "Where did I leave my keys?"

How Liuyao Works

Liuyao divination follows a structured process: you formulate a specific question, cast six coins (or use yarrow stalks), and derive six lines from which a hexagram emerges. Each line exists in relation to the others, and a skilled Liuyao reader interprets not just the hexagram as a whole, but the dynamic relationships between each line — which lines are "empty" (unstable), which are "full" (settled), and which are "changing" (in flux).

The Core Principle

Everything in the universe operates on the principle of Yin and Yang — complementary forces in constant motion. Liuyao captures the precise state of these forces at the moment you ask your question, revealing the likely outcome based on current energy patterns.

Reading the Six Lines

Each of the six lines in a Liuyao hexagram represents a different layer of your question:

  • Line 1 (Bottom) — The foundation, initial conditions, how things begin
  • Line 2 — First response, early developments, secondary factors
  • Line 3 — Lower middle, a critical juncture, caution required
  • Line 4 — Upper middle, internal dynamics, personal factors
  • Line 5 — Near top, external pressure, public perception, senior figures
  • Line 6 (Top) — The outcome zone, final result, long-term implications

Lines that change (animated by the cast) become "virtual" lines — revealing what the situation would become if current trends continue unchecked. The unchanged lines show what remains stable. Together, they tell a complete story from present to future.

Practical Applications

Business Decisions

Should I take this partnership? Is the timing right to launch? Liuyao reveals the hidden dynamics around business moves with surprising specificity.

Relationship Questions

What does this person truly feel? Is this relationship going somewhere? Liuyao cuts through confusion to the core energy of interpersonal situations.

Career and Migration

Should I take this job offer? Is relocation the right move? Liuyao shows the energetic trajectory of major life decisions.

Lost Items and People

Where did I leave it? Can I find what I've lost? Liuyao has a long history of providing precise, verifiable answers to location questions.

The Liuyao Process: Step by Step

  1. Clarify your question — Liuyao requires a single, specific question. "Should I invest in this" works; "Should I invest, and what about the house?" does not.
  2. Focus and cast — Hold your question clearly in mind, then cast six coins (or stalks) simultaneously. Repeat three times.
  3. Record the six lines — Each cast produces one line, building from bottom to top. Note which lines are changing.
  4. Read the hexagram — Your six lines map to one of 64 hexagrams, each with traditional wisdom and interpretation.
  5. Interpret dynamically — A skilled reader analyzes line relationships, empty/full states, and changing lines to give a nuanced prediction.

Key Principle: Current Energy

Liuyao captures the energy of "now" — not fate in an absolute sense, but the most likely outcome given current trajectories. If you act on the advice and change your approach, the outcome can shift accordingly. Liuyao is a weather forecast for your decision landscape, not a verdict.

Liuyao vs. Other Systems

Liuyao differs from other Chinese divination methods in important ways:

  • vs. BaZi: BaZi is a natal chart — it reveals who you are by birth. Liuyao is situational — it answers what is happening right now in a specific context.
  • vs. Meihua: Meihua is faster and uses any number source (time, sound, image). Liuyao uses a structured coin-stalk ritual and produces a more complete hexagram with richer line-level detail.
  • vs. Ziwei: Ziwei maps life cycles and timing over years. Liuyao answers immediate questions with precision — faster but narrower in scope.

The ideal approach is to use multiple systems: BaZi for long-term direction, Ziwei for life-area analysis, and Liuyao when you need a specific, immediate answer. Each illuminates a different dimension of the same underlying truth.
