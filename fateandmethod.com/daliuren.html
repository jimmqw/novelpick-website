Daliuren

Big Six Cycle — one of the three great Chinese divination systems, focused on time-based energy analysis for strategic decisions.

Daliuren (大六壬, "Big Six Cycle") is one of the three most respected systems in classical Chinese divination — alongside Qimen Dunjia and Liuyao. It is a time-based method that analyzes the energy configuration at the precise moment you ask your question, treating time itself as the primary data source. The system is particularly renowned for strategic applications: military decisions, official matters, travel planning, and any situation where timing and positioning are critical.

The Three Great Divination Systems

Classical Chinese divination is often grouped into the "Three Great Systems" (三大数):

  • Daliuren (大六壬) — Time-focused, excellent for strategic and official matters
  • Liutiange (六壬神课) — Liuyao's broader hexagram system for specific question answering
  • Qimen Dunjia (奇门遁甲) — Space and timing system for optimal action placement

Of the three, Daliuren is considered particularly precise for questions involving multiple parties, timing conflicts, and situations where knowing who moves first matters.

How Daliuren Works

Daliuren extracts its reading from the time of the question — not just the hour, but the full temporal energy configuration including the cyclical relationships between the stems and branches active at that moment. It then maps these energies onto a structured chart that reveals:

Primary Actors

The main parties involved in the question — represented by the key stems and branches active in the moment.

Power Dynamics

Which party has the stronger energy at this moment — who holds the initiative, who is on the defensive.

Timing

When events will accelerate, when momentum will shift, and the overall timeline of the situation.

Strategic Advice

What action to take — advance, wait, deflect — based on the energy map of the current moment.

Daliuren vs. Liuyao

Liuyao uses a coin-cast hexagram and is excellent for specific questions. Daliuren takes time as its primary input and maps energy relationships more systematically — better suited for multi-party situations and timing-based decisions. Daliuren requires more learning but offers greater strategic depth.

Best Applications

  • Official and career matters — promotions, disputes with authorities, government interactions
  • Legal and contractual situations — who has the stronger position, when to proceed
  • Travel and relocation — timing and direction analysis
  • Multi-party negotiations — understanding power dynamics and optimal positioning
  • Strategic planning — when to act, when to wait, where to direct energy
