Books Like Solo Leveling – Top 15 Web Novels You Must Read

If you devoured Solo Leveling and are desperately hunting for more, this list is for you.

📅 April 2026 ⏱️ 12 min read ✍️ NovelPick Editorial

Solo Leveling carved its place as one of the most beloved web novels and manhwa of the past decade. Its "hunter who levels up alone" premise, punishing dungeons, and Sung Jinwoo's satisfying underdog-to-overpowered arc set a benchmark that dozens of series have tried to emulate. Whether you're looking for the same dungeon-crawling adrenaline, a protagonist who starts weak and ascends to godhood, or that perfect mix of action and slow-burn power fantasy — here are 15 series that deliver.

The Appeal of Solo Leveling: Why We Can't Stop Reading It

Before diving into recommendations, it helps to understand what makes Solo Leveling work so well. The core appeal lies in several pillars: the system-based progression (levels, stats, skills, dungeons), the relentless upward momentum of the protagonist's power, high-stakes combat against creative monster designs, and a supporting cast that grows alongside the hero without overshadowing him. The art in the manhwa adaptation — by Chugong (story) and DUBU (art) — is cinematic and gorgeous. If a series hits even two of these pillars, it's usually worth your time.

The Top 15 Books & Novels Like Solo Leveling

#1The Beginning After The End
by TurtleMe · Web Novel & Manhwa

King Grey had everything — power, wealth, looks — yet felt utterly empty. Reborn into a magical world as a child named Arthur Leywin with his memories intact, he must navigate a new life where strength is measured in mana and dungeons are a fact of life. This series ticks nearly every Solo Leveling box: system-like magic progression, dungeons, a protagonist who grows from gifted child to unstoppable force, and absolutely stunning manhwa art. Its emotional depth — exploring loneliness, identity, and what it means to be truly alive — gives it an edge over pure power-fantasy series. Consider it the spiritual successor and most complete package on this list.

Isekai Magic System Dungeons Coming-of-age
#2Omniscient Reader's Viewpoint
Web Novel & Manhwa · By Sing Shong

Imagine if the web novel you'd been reading for 10 years suddenly became reality — and you were the only person who remembered it. Kim Dokja is that reader, and the "scenario" that has destroyed most of humanity is the very story he loved. ORV is a genre-bending masterpiece that deconstructs the power-fantasy tropes Solo Leveling plays straight. Its combat is spectacular, its mythology is staggeringly deep, and the relationship between Kim Dokja and Yoo Jonghyuk is one of the most compelling dynamics in all of web fiction. The manhwa art is arguably even better than Solo Leveling's in terms of dynamic action sequences.

Regression Metafiction Apocalypse Ensemble Cast
#3Second Life Ranker
Web Novel · By Sadoyeon

When Yeon-woo's twin brother disappears after discovering a mysterious pocket watch, Yeon-woo inherits it — and with it, the ability to enter the Tower, a multiversal structure where climbers fight, level up, and gain power across countless worlds. The comparison to Solo Leveling is obvious and intentional, but Second Life Ranker does its own thing well: the combat is intricate, the world-building across multiple tower floors is genuinely creative, and Yeon-woo's methodical, vengeful climb feels personal and driven. If you want more of that "climb the tower alone" feeling, this is your direct hit.

Tower Climbing Revenge Story Multiverse Combat
#4Return of the Mount Hua Sect
Web Novel & Manhwa · By Biga

This is the rare wuxia/xianxia entry that crossover audiences love. Chung Myung is a legendary swordsman from the Mount Hua Sect who dies in battle — and wakes up centuries later as a young disciple with his memories intact. What follows is part redemption arc, part comedy, and entirely a revenge story against those who destroyed his sect. The humor is surprisingly good for a series about ancient martial arts politics, and the sword combat is choreographed with real flair in the manhwa. Think Solo Leveling's "oppressed hero claws back to glory" energy, but in a wuxia setting with monks, swords, and internal energy cultivation.

Wuxia Reincarnation Sword Combat Humorous
#5Overlord
Light Novel & Anime/Manga · By Kugane Maruyama

The "what if the villain was the protagonist" answer to isekai. Momonga, a skeletal-overlord DMT (dungeon master tool) from a YGGDRASIL DMO (the in-world game), is trapped in his avatar's body in a world that's both his game and terrifyingly real. He retains his power, his NPC minions, and his guild base — and sets out to conquer the world while the narrative deliberately asks whether he's still the player he was. Overlord flips Solo Leveling's premise: instead of the underdog becoming the strongest, the strongest existing being explores a world where everyone else is weak. Dark, strategic, and deeply compelling.

Isekai Villain Protagonist Dark Fantasy Strategy
#6The Legendary Moonlight Sculptor
Web Novel · By Nam Heesung

Weirdly underappreciated despite being one of the most popular Korean web novels ever published. The MC, Lee Hyun, is a poor student who escapes into the VRMMORPG Royal Road — where he becomes an adventurer known as the legendary "Dark Fall." What makes this series special is its detailed, almost economic-sim approach to progression: crafting, farming gold, building a guild, managing in-game politics. It's slower-paced than Solo Leveling but rewards patience with one of the richest fictional game worlds in the genre. The manhwa adaptation is solid, though the novel goes much deeper.

VRMMORPG Crafting Game World Strategy
#7Solo Leveling's True Clone: Tomb Raider King
Manhwa · By Son Jaeguen

Let's be upfront: this is Solo Leveling with a tomb-raiding skin. Chu Jooheon, a "tomb raider" who explores ancient dungeons, is given a second chance at life after dying in a dungeon collapse. He awakens with a "tomb rating system" that lets him judge and absorb the power of other tombs. The progression, dungeon structure, and art style are unmistakably inspired by Solo Leveling — so much so that it's almost a tribute. If you're specifically hungry for "more Solo Leveling, right now, with minimal learning curve," this will scratch that itch. It doesn't innovate much, but it's entertaining.

Dungeons System Tomb Raiding Power Fantasy
#8A Returner's Magic Should Be Special
Web Novel & Manhwa · By Usonan

Deson Arma is a failed magician from the Shadow Labyrinth — a top-tier dungeon — who gets sent back in time after the world's catastrophic end. With foreknowledge of the magic system, dungeon layouts, and the weakness of future enemies, he enrolls in the same academy he once failed out of, this time playing a long game. The "academy arc" that follows is actually engaging rather than filler, which is rare. Deson's strategic use of his knowledge — rather than raw power alone — gives the series a different flavor from Solo Leveling. The art in the manhwa is colorful and expressive.

Regression Academy Magic System Strategy
#9The Great Mage Returns After 4000 Years
Web Novel & Manhwa · By Barnacle

The weakest student in the Korean Fantasy Academy's history, Lukas Forge, turns out to be the legendary "Great Mage" Freydoon — sealed away 4,000 years ago and reborn in the body of his own descendant. When the seal breaks and his full power awakens, he has to navigate a world that has spent millennia forgetting magic existed. This is Solo Leveling's "hidden OP power" trope done with a wizard instead of a hunter, and it works beautifully. The world-building around ancient magic civilizations gives it a more epic scope, and Lukas is a fantastically dry, competent protagonist.

Reincarnation Ancient Magic Academy Hidden Power
#10Hardcore Leveling Warrior
Web Novel & Manhwa · By Kkulbee

If Solo Leveling is the grimdark version of the "hunter who levels up" story, Hardcore Leveling Warrior is the absurdist comedy send-up that somehow also has genuinely excellent combat. Warrior Gildong — yes, like the Korean folk hero — was the #1 player in the KBT (Kill the Beast) game until he was betrayed and stripped of everything. Now he has to claw his way back to the top while the game starts bleeding into reality. It's chaotic, funny, surprisingly emotional, and the manhwa art is absolutely spectacular — some of the best panel work in the entire manhwa space.

Comedy VRMMORPG Fallen Hero Gaming
#11Trash of the Count's Family
Web Novel & Manhwa · By Yoo Ryeo Han

Cale Henituse is a shameless, lazy trash who drinks away his noble family's fortune — except he's actually Choi Han, a transmigrator who possesses the body of a novel's side character and knows the plot. He deliberately plays the role of the trash to avoid getting involved in the original story's tragic events, but his interference slowly unravels everything. This is a beloved series that takes a while to get going but builds into something genuinely great. Cale's "I'll do the bare minimum and somehow everything works out" approach is refreshingly different from the grim determination of typical Solo Leveling protagonists.

Reincarnation Villain Deuteragonist Political Intrigue Slow Burn
#12Reincarnation of the Suicidal Battle God
Web Novel & Manhwa · By 91戫

A Murim/xianxia crossover that delivers relentless combat and an unusually poignant protagonist. Zephyr, the "Battle God" of the Portal Knights, is killed in battle and reincarnated 200 years in the past as a weak young man named Ford. With his god-level combat instincts and knowledge of the future, he sets out to change destiny — and protect the people he failed the first time. The martial arts are intricate and satisfying, the power-ups are earned, and Ford's quiet determination to protect the weak gives the series real emotional weight. Extremely underrated.

Reincarnation Murim Combat Martial Arts
#13Nano Machine
Web Novel & Manhwa · By Qie Mo Tu

In a future where martial arts survive alongside advanced nanotechnology, hand-to-hand combat and sword qi are augmented by powered-up nanobots — and the "Nano Machine" itself is a system given to the protagonist, Yung Mun-ah, a lowborn child in a powerful family. This fusion of cultivation/xianxia tropes with sci-fi tech is genuinely unique. The martial arts choreography is spectacular, the politics are intricate, and the protagonist's progression from outcast to peak power is deeply satisfying. If you want something that's "Solo Leveling meets Crouching Tiger" — this is it.

Sci-Fi Martial Arts Xianxia Nanotech Underdog
#14Legend of the Northern Blade
Manhwa & Web Novel · By Yoo Seongmin

While not strictly a system-based progression like Solo Leveling, Legend of the Northern Blade delivers the same kind of breathtaking single-combat satisfaction. Jin Kihyun, the sole survivor of the Northern Blade Sect after it was destroyed by demonic martial artists, sets out alone on a path of revenge across a martial arts world teeming with powerful enemies. The art is arguably the best on this list — each fight scene is choreographed and rendered with cinematic precision. If you're coming to this list primarily for incredible action art, start here.

Wuxia Revenge Stunning Art Solo Protagonist
#15Weak Hero Class 1
Web Novel & Manhwa · By Seopass

The most grounded entry on this list, and arguably one of the best. Alex Benetis is academically brilliant but physically unremarkable — until he attends a school where brute force dominates the social hierarchy. What follows is a series of increasingly brutal fights as Alex uses his intellect, leverage, and surprising physical adaptation to defeat opponents far bigger and stronger than himself. It's not a dungeon-crawler or isekai — there's no magic system, no levels, no monsters. But if your love for Solo Leveling was rooted in the "underdog who wins through brain and will" formula, Weak Hero Class 1 delivers that high in a completely different setting. The manhwa art is also exceptional.

School Action Realistic Fighting Underdog Intellect + Will

Honorable Mentions

If you've burned through all 15 and still want more, here are a few more series worth exploring: The Reborn Young Lord Is an Assassin (revenge assassin regression story with gorgeous art), My S-Class Hunters (reverse-harem hunter story with surprisingly good combat), Seoul Station's Necromancer (necromancer protagonist in an urban fantasy game world), and Doctor Elves Can't Be So Cute (lighter tone, healer protagonist with dungeon-crawling).

Which Should You Read First?

If you want the closest Solo Leveling experience with even better world-building: start with The Beginning After The End. If you want something that deconstructs the genre and is intellectually richer: Omniscient Reader's Viewpoint. If you want pure dungeon-climbing dopamine: Second Life Ranker. And if you want the best action art in the manhwa space: Legend of the Northern Blade.

The beauty of this golden age of Korean web fiction is that there's genuinely something for everyone — and most of these series are available free on platforms like Webnovel, Tappytoon, and Manta. Happy reading.
