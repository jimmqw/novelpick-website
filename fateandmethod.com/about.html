About Fate and Method

Making two thousand years of Chinese metaphysical wisdom accessible, practical, and accurate for the modern reader.

Fate and Method exists to bridge the gap between the ancient wisdom of Chinese metaphysics and the modern seeker who wants practical, actionable guidance for life decisions, relationships, career, and personal understanding.

What We Cover

We focus on the eight core systems of Chinese metaphysics — BaZi (Four Pillars), Ziwei Dou Shou (Purple Star Astrology), Liuyao (Six Lines Divination), Meihua Yishu (Plum Blossom Numerology), Daliuren (Big Six Cycle), Xuankong (Flying Star Feng Shui), Taiyi Liuren (Divine Numbers), and Xiaoliuren (Little Six Cycle). Each system has its own domain of application, its own methodology, and its own depth. Together, they form the most complete framework for understanding human experience and the energetic currents that shape it.

We are not making claims of supernatural accuracy or mystical authority. Chinese metaphysics is a systematic study of cyclical patterns in time and space — it works because the universe operates on discoverable principles of change and balance. We present it as what it is: an elegant, tested framework for understanding dynamics that are otherwise difficult to perceive.

Editorial Standards

Every article on Fate and Method is written to be clear, accurate, and practically useful. We distinguish clearly between:

  • Factual content — the mechanics of each system, how calculations work, what symbols mean
  • Traditional interpretations — what practitioners over centuries have observed about star combinations, element interactions, and timing patterns
  • Analytical guidance — our editorial perspective on how to interpret and apply the systems in modern life

Our Approach

We believe that metaphysics should serve daily life, not occupy a separate spiritual compartment from it. The goal is not to become dependent on divination — it is to develop enough awareness of the energetic landscape that you make better decisions, time your actions more effectively, and understand yourself and others more deeply.

Whether you come to Chinese metaphysics for the first time or have been studying for years, we aim to provide content that is both foundational and substantive — worth reading whether you are a beginner or an experienced practitioner.
